AI4RA at REACH 2026

AI4RA: The Intersection Between AI and Data

A workshop for research administration professionals who need a practical way to think about prompting, evaluation, context, automation, and when AI should support human work instead of obscuring it.

Why this workshop exists

Practical AI adoption starts with judgment, not novelty.

Artificial intelligence and data science are transforming research administration and other higher education functions, but the value is uneven unless people can distinguish useful application from hype. This session frames AI and data science as complementary tools, shows where human expertise remains essential, and gives participants a grounded way to design reviewable workflows rather than black-box experiments.

Learning objectives

What participants should leave able to do

Prompting

Improve output quality

Apply prompt engineering fundamentals to improve quality, relevance, and consistency in AI responses.

Decomposition

Break work into parts

Turn complex research administration tasks into discrete, AI-manageable components with review points.

Workflow design

Build end-to-end workflows

Develop complete AI-assisted workflows for common research administration processes.

Judgment

Decide what belongs

Evaluate which tasks and workflows are suitable for AI deployment in participants' own institutions.

Workshop flow

Three hours organized around decision quality

The session moves from framing and task selection into prompting, context design, and demonstrations so attendees can connect ideas directly to real research administration work.

1:00 PM

Foundations

AI versus data science, actionable human-centered applications, and lessons from the AI4RA NSF GRANTED project.

1:45 PM

Prompting and evaluation

Prompt engineering, structured output, and judging whether a response is trustworthy and usable.

2:30 PM

Context and retrieval

CLI, files, images, MCP tools, RAG, SQL, semantic search, and unstructured data as workflow inputs.

3:15 PM

Demos and discussion

Promptulus, Vandalizer, UDM feedback, automation fit, and guardrails for vibe coding.

Topic brainstorm

Priority threads for the session

These are the content lanes that make the workshop practical for participants who need to return to their institutions and decide what to pilot, what to document, and what to reject.

  • Which tasks are suitable for AI and automation in layman-friendly terms
  • Efficiency augmentation and guardrails for responsible vibe coding
  • RAG, semantic search, extraction, and unstructured data workflows
  • Kinds of context: CLI, files, images, MCP tools, RAG, and SQL
  • Response evaluation and judging AI output quality
  • Structured output for repeatable workflows and downstream automation
