Learning objectives

What participants should leave able to do

AI literacy

Understand what AI can and cannot do for research analytics

Explain how large language models work at a practical level, apply FAIR principles as operational guardrails, and distinguish AI capabilities from institutional judgment.

Prompt engineering

Structure prompts that produce reliable, consistent output

Move from ad-hoc prompting to deliberate prompt design using role instructions, examples, templates, and constraints that improve quality and reduce drift.

Data readiness

Evaluate whether institutional data is ready for AI

Assess provenance, permissions, currency, and explainability of data sources before connecting them to AI workflows. Distinguish authoritative sources from supplemental ones and identify governance gaps.

Data governance

Govern AI inputs and outputs with the same rigor as dashboards

Recognize that AI both consumes governed data and generates new data products. Apply stewardship, permissioning, and traceability standards to both sides.

Context architecture

Choose the right layer of context for the job

Map the context stack — instructions, templates, files, tools, retrieval, and human escalation — and use the thinnest layer that solves the problem reliably.

Evaluation

Verify AI output before it enters a workflow

Apply reproducibility, accuracy, and trust checks to AI-generated outputs. Decide when the system should answer directly, cite and defer, or escalate to a human before action is taken.

Adoption judgment

Decide what to automate, augment, or leave alone

Classify institutional tasks by AI suitability. Recognize when a well-designed query, dashboard, or human process is the better answer — and when AI adds genuine value beyond existing tools.
