Module 1 — Led by Nathan Wiggins

The intersection between AI and data science.

This opening module gives participants shared language and a practical baseline before deeper technical modules. It connects AI4RA project goals, FAIR principles, Vandalizer workflows, and model framing choices to real research analytics work.

Back to course hub Launch slide deck

Module brief

Establish shared foundations before advanced implementation.

Lead

Nathan Wiggins

SPARC Data Scientist, Southern Utah University

Duration

50 minutes

Four parts: AI4RA Introduction, Data Science & AI Intersection, Sandbox Time, and Decision Frameworks.

Outcome

Shared language and orientation

Participants can map institutional needs to AI4RA framing and identify opportunities to apply data science and artificial intelligence tools to solving business problems.

Learning objectives

What participants should leave able to do

  • Understand the evolving reach of data science in research analytics, apply FAIR principles as operational guardrails, and determine appropriate levels of human-in-the-loop intervention.
  • Apply frameworks based on foundational principles to make decisions in one's own institutional context regarding the implementation of emerging technologies.

Teaching flow

Module parts and facilitation sequence

Part 1 · 10 minutes

AI4RA Introduction

  • Objectives, principal investigators, and pillars: accuracy, reproducibility, flexibility, security.
  • Community of practice orientation: Vandalizer, Data Lake, and related projects.
  • Bridge to research analytics: reducing barriers to high-level strategic analysis.
Part 2 · 10 minutes

Data Science & AI Intersection

  • FAIR principles as operational guardrails for AI-enabled workflows.
  • Transparent FAIR citation: Wilkinson et al., Scientific Data (2016), https://doi.org/10.1038/sdata.2016.18.
  • Proactive data science: shift in our reach.
  • Reactive data science: shift in their values.
Part 3 · 20 minutes

Sandbox Time

  • Hands-on Vandalizer walkthrough and live workspace orientation.
  • Exercise sequence: simple extraction, repeated extractions, and multi-document interactions.
  • Facilitator support while participants practice prompt-driven workflows.
Part 4 · 10 minutes

Decision Frameworks

  • Evaluate human-led, human-in-the-loop, and AI-led task zones.
  • Compare evolving task fit across data science and AI workflows.
  • Apply intent, information, instructions, and conversation disciplines.

Facilitation support

Closing the module

Demo handoff

Promptulus demo closes this session

End with a live Promptulus demo showing how prompt structure affects output quality. Then pose the bridge question: "But what about the data underneath?" — handing off directly to Nathan Layman's session on data organization.
