Module 1 — Led by Nathan Wiggins

Foundational framing for AI4RA and institutional AI practice.

This opening module gives participants shared language and a practical baseline before deeper technical modules. It connects AI4RA project goals, FAIR principles, Vandalizer workflows, and model framing choices to real research analytics work.

Back to course hub Launch slide deck

Module brief

Establish shared foundations before advanced implementation.

Lead

Nathan Wiggins

Primary facilitator for module framing, demo walkthrough, and Q&A.

Duration

45 minutes

Five parts: framing, principles, demo, discussion, and model/task frameworks.

Outcome

Shared language and orientation

Participants can map institutional needs to AI4RA framing and identify next practical workflow steps.

Teaching flow

Module parts and facilitation sequence

Part 1 · 10 minutes

AI4RA Introduction

  • Objectives, principal investigators, and pillars: accuracy, reproducibility, flexibility, security.
  • Community of practice orientation: Vandalizer, Data Lake, and related projects.
  • Bridge to research analytics: reducing barriers to high-level strategic analysis.
Part 2 · 10 minutes

Intersection of Data Science and Artificial Intelligence

  • FAIR principles as operational guardrails for AI-enabled workflows.
  • Proactive data science: shift in our reach.
  • Reactive data science: shift in their values.
Part 3 · 10 minutes

Vandalizer Workflow Demo

  • Contrast Vandalizer with other UI patterns and expectations.
  • Highlight the document interface and how context is managed.
  • Show workflows that operationalize AI in institutional settings.
Part 4 · 5 minutes

Vandalizer Question / Answer

  • Invite practical implementation and governance questions.
  • Capture blockers participants see in their own environments.
Part 5 · 10 minutes

Frameworks and Models

  • Data science and AI tasks as the pathway toward agentic workflows.
  • Prompt engineering evolves toward intent engineering.
  • The only constant: maintain a strong and trustworthy data foundation.

Facilitation support

Bridge to Session 2

Demo handoff

Promptulus demo closes this session

End with a live Promptulus demo showing how prompt structure affects output quality. Then pose the bridge question: "But what about the data underneath?" — handing off directly to Nathan Layman's session on data organization.
