Module 1 — Led by Nathan Wiggins

The intersection between AI and data science

This session is your strategic launchpad for the rest of REACH 2026. You will leave with a practical, research-analytics-first framework for deciding where AI accelerates institutional work, where human accountability must stay central, and how to move from experimentation to trustworthy implementation.

Why this session matters

Immediate value for research analytics teams

Total time

50 minutes

Four focused parts with a clear throughline: from strategy to hands-on practice to decision governance.

Who this helps most

Research analytics professionals

Ideal for teams supporting research data, compliance, leadership reporting, and institutional planning.

Primary outcome

A repeatable AI decision model

You will be able to scope tasks, identify safe automation opportunities, and keep policy-relevant work reviewable.

What to apply next week

High-impact workflow upgrades

  • Reduce cycle time on extraction and synthesis tasks from funding, compliance, and award documentation.
  • Use FAIR-aligned data handling as a practical guardrail for AI-supported analytics workflows.
  • Classify work by auditability need (human-led vs human-in-the-loop vs AI-led) before deployment.

Module structure

What happens in each part

Part 1 · 10 min

AI4RA Introduction

  • Anchor to AI4RA objectives: open-source workflows, trustworthy AI tools, and measurable partner impact.
  • Frame the four pillars that make analytics adoption credible: accuracy, reproducibility, flexibility, and security.
  • Connect the community ecosystem (Vandalizer, data lakehouse, AI literacy resources) to institutional delivery capacity.
  • Translate this foundation into a core promise: fewer barriers to meaningful strategic analysis.
Part 2 · 10 min

Data Science & AI Intersection

  • Use FAIR principles (Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, Reusable) as operational guardrails for AI-enabled work.
  • Compare two simultaneous shifts: proactive data science (what teams can now build) and reactive data science (what stakeholders now expect).
  • Demystify LLM behavior through token-by-token generation so output variability feels manageable, not mysterious.
  • Apply a simple three-step execution pattern: identify a bottleneck, classify the task, build the least intimidating solution.
Part 3 · 20 min

Sandbox Time

  • Enter the Vandalizer workspace and move quickly from concept to practical workflow trials.
  • Exercise 1: run targeted extraction from a funding opportunity document.
  • Exercise 2: repeat extraction across multiple award files to test process consistency and resilience.
  • Exercise 3: compare multiple documents to detect misalignment and support analytic quality control.
Part 4 · 10 min

Decision Frameworks

  • Review authorship and ownership risk in AI-assisted content, especially where institutional or sponsor constraints apply.
  • Place analytic tasks on the human-led ↔ AI-led continuum based on accountability, not hype.
  • Update execution mindset from prompt engineering alone to intent engineering and workflow fit.
  • Apply four interaction disciplines (intent, information, instructions, conversation) to make better use of limited context windows.
